Sampling an Application

  1. Connect your target device to your computer and power it on (or wake it up).
    If your device is not listed when typing adb devices from the command line, it will not be accessible for sampling.
  2. Launch the Tegra System Profiler application.
  3. A new Project will be displayed docked in the interface:

  4. If you have more than one device attached to your computer, use the Device drop-down list in the top-left to select your device. Details for the selected device are shown below the combo box.
  5. Select the application to sample. If you have already installed your application on the device, choose the Application option and select your application from the combo box:


    The list also includes a PID column, so you can see whether or not the process is already running. Below are checkboxes that control the attach and relaunch behaviors. By disabling both checkboxes, the behavior is a pure attach, with risk of triggering potential behavior changes due to processes and intent.

    Alternatively, if you have an APK on your computer, and would like deploy it to the device for sampling, click the "Install application from APK file" link, located above the application list, and it will pop up a standard file selection dialog. The APK will be installed, and then you may select it in the application list.

  6. Below the application area is a collapsible area for symbols. Some APKs will have their symbols built into the *.so files but most will not. Instead, right-click on this list, and add a directory pointing at the version of the *.so files in your APK that are not stripped of symbols. Typically this is the output of the native build stage, before it is copied into the APK packaging directory and then stripped there. Note: if you do not provide symbols, then functions will appear with their hexadecimal address instead of its proper name, significantly reducing the usefulness of the tool.
  7. Press the Next button in the upper-right corner, to switch to the Analysis Options setup page.
  8. Select the sampling rate and hardware counters you would like to sample. Turn on Collect Call Stacks to calculate backtraces for each sample (at a significant impact to sampling performance). The higher the rate, the more data you will generate and the more impact sampling will have on your application. We recommend 10 kHz for short sessions of 30 seconds or less, as long sessions may terminate due to fast buffering requirements.

    NOTE: We are currently seeing occasional OS-level freezes when sampling for a long time or at a high sampling rate. If you find your hardware becomes non-responsive during sampling, you may wish to try lowering the sampling rate, or uncheck the Collect Call Stacks option.

    NOTE: Some hardware does not support sampling more than one L2 Cache counter in a single session. (You will get an error before sampling begins if you select two or more L2 Cache counters and this is the case.)
  9. Choose whether you wish to start capturing immediately or after a predefined period of time, and whether you wish to stop sampling on demand or after a predefined period of sampling.
  10. Press Start.
  11. Press the Stop sampling button when you have exercised your application sufficiently.


    For details on understanding and analyzing the sampling report, see the section Reports.
